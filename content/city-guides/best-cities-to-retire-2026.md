## Best Cities to Retire in 2026

### Penang, Malaysia – Calm, Affordable, and Expat-Friendly

Penang has its own unique rhythm. Sometimes you hear the soft sounds of the city, sometimes the smell of local dishes fills the air, and sometimes the ocean breeze gives you a deep sense of peace and freedom.

Life here moves at a slower, more enjoyable pace. Locals are friendly, welcoming, and many speak English fluently, making it easy for retirees to integrate.

Apartments typically range from $300 to $800 per month, depending on comfort level. Healthcare is strong, modern, and affordable. The tropical climate is warm year-round, with occasional rain that most people quickly adapt to.

Malaysia is a Muslim country, but it is also one of the most open and modern in Asia. Penang has a strong expat community, especially among Americans and Canadians, making it easy to build a social life.

---

### Hua Hin, Thailand – Simple Living and True Freedom

Thailand is often described as a country of freedom, and Hua Hin reflects that perfectly.

Unlike more crowded tourist destinations, Hua Hin is peaceful, clean, and well-organized. It offers a lifestyle similar to coastal cities like Florida, but at a fraction of the cost.

Daily life is incredibly affordable. Services like cleaning cost as little as $10–$20 per week, and high-speed internet is available for around $17 per month.

Healthcare is one of the strongest advantages. Hospitals are modern, efficient, and affordable, often considered among the best value in the world.

With beaches, palm trees, and a growing expat community, Hua Hin offers a relaxed and fulfilling retirement lifestyle.

---

### Benidorm, Spain – Mediterranean Lifestyle at Lower Cost

Do you want to live in a place similar to Miami but at half the cost? Benidorm offers exactly that.

This vibrant coastal city is popular among retirees from the US and the UK. It combines modern living with Mediterranean charm, warm weather, and a highly social environment.

While it is more expensive than Southeast Asia, it is still very affordable compared to most of the US and Western Europe.

The city offers beaches, parks, golf clubs, and a lively expat scene. The old town adds a traditional Spanish touch with narrow streets, historic architecture, and local culture.

---

### Mendoza, Argentina – Nature, Wine, and Relaxed Living

Mendoza is a unique destination surrounded by the Andes Mountains, offering breathtaking landscapes and a peaceful lifestyle.

This city is ideal for retirees who value nature, calmness, and culture. It is known for its world-class wine industry, with vineyards spread across the region.

Healthcare is high-quality and affordable, especially in private clinics. Daily expenses, including restaurants and services, are very reasonable.

With sunny weather, friendly locals, and a slower pace of life, Mendoza is one of the best options in South America for a relaxed retirement.

---

### Montevideo, Uruguay – Stability and High Quality of Life

Montevideo is widely considered one of the safest and most stable cities in South America.

The city offers a European feel, with architecture similar to Spain and Portugal. It is clean, organized, and known for its strong banking system and political stability.

One of its highlights is the Rambla — a long coastal boulevard where people walk, cycle, and enjoy outdoor life daily.

While it is one of the more expensive cities in the region, around $2,000 per month can still provide a comfortable lifestyle — below what many couples allocate in our [best cities for a $3,000/month budget](/city-guides/best-cities-for-3000-month-budget-2026) guide.

Healthcare is excellent, and the overall quality of life is among the highest in Latin America.
